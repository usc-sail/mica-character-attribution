This qualification task consists of two parts. In the first part, you will answer four questions about movie characters. In the second part, you will indicate which movies you have watched from a list of about 200 movies, all released after 2010.


First Part: Yes/No Questions

A trope is a storytelling device or convention the storyteller uses to describe situations the audience can easily recognize. Think of them as character attributes.

For each of the four questions, observe the character shown and the definition of the trope. You can click on the character page link if you are not able to identify the character from their name and the pictures. Then choose yes/no to answer whether the character portrays the trope. If you are not familiar with the character, please consult online sites like Wikipedia and Fandom Wikis, read about the character and then answer.

Second Part: Movie Survey

The second part of the task is a movie survey. Choose only the movies you have watched. Kindly do not spam select all the movies. We will screen in workers who have watched a subset of the movies.


Based upon your answers and movie selections, you would be qualified to perform a question-answering task about movie characters similar to the four questions in the first part of this task.

Please reach out to sailusc.mturk@gmail.com for any questions.

Question 1

Marta Cabrera is a character that appears in the movie Knives Out (Character Page).

The GoodIsNotDumb trope involves a genuinely good and kind character underestimated by others, who mistakenly see them as naive or foolish. However, their goodness prevails, proving that being kind and trusting does not equate to being gullible or weak. Go to GoodIsNotDumb to learn more about it.

Does Marta Cabrera portray or is associated with the GoodIsNotDumb trope?


Question 2

Max Rockatansky is a character that appears in the movie Mad Max: Fury Road (Character Page).

The LoveInterest trope refers to a character who becomes romantically involved with another, such as a princess to be rescued or a Prince Charming. It is a universal super-trope encompassing various specific romantic scenarios. Go to LoveInterest to learn more about it.

Does Max Rockatansky portray or is associated with the LoveInterest trope?


Question 3

Maleficent is a character that appears in the movie Maleficent (Character Page).

The ScreamingWoman trope involves a female character reacting to terrifying danger by standing helplessly and screaming, often requiring rescue by a hero. This can be frustrating, especially for characters expected to be strong or capable. Go to ScreamingWoman to learn more about it.

Does Maleficent portray or is associated with the ScreamingWoman trope?


Question 4

Belle is a character that appears in the movie Beauty and the Beast (Character Page).

The UnwittingInstigatorOfDoom trope describes a character who unintentionally plays a key role in causing a major disaster or catastrophe, often without understanding their impact or being manipulated without realizing it. Go to UnwittingInstigatorOfDoom to learn more about it.

Does Belle portray or is associated with the UnwittingInstigatorOfDoom trope?
